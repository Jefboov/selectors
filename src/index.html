How to use in 2 words

Download and install Source Filmmaker from the Steam Store.It's a free software developed by Valve Corporation.

Once you've installed Source Filmmaker, open the software and select "New Session" to create a new project.

Choose the game that you want to work with, such as Team Fortress 2 or Half-Life 2, and select the map you want to use.

Choose your characters, props, and other assets from the Asset Browser on the right side of the screen. You can drag and
drop these items onto the map or into the Animation Set Editor.

To animate your characters, select one of the Animation Set Editors (one for each character or prop you add) and click the
"Record" button. You can then move the character around and add keyframes to create animation.

To add sound effects or dialogue, you can import audio files and place them on the timeline in the Motion Editor. You can also
use the Audio Clip Editor to adjust the volume and timing of your sound effects.

When you're finished with your animation, you can export it as a video file by selecting "Export Movie" from the File menu.
Choose your export settings, such as resolution and format, and click "Export" to save your file.

A step-by-step guide on how to animate and work in Source Filmmaker (SFM)

Set up your project: Open SFM and create a new session. Choose the game you want to work with and the map you want to use.

Add assets to your scene: Use the Asset Browser on the right side of the screen to find and add characters, props, and other assets to your scene. Drag and drop them onto the map or into the Animation Set Editor.

Create an animation: Double-click on an asset in the Animation Set Editor to open its timeline. Click the "Record" button to start recording animation. Move the asset around the scene and add keyframes to create your animation.

You can adjust the timing and interpolation of your keyframes by selecting them and using the handles to manipulate them.

Add audio: Use the Audio Clip Editor to import audio files and add them to your animation. You can adjust the volume and timing of your audio clips to match your animation.

Fine-tune your animation: Use the Graph Editor to fine-tune the motion of your assets. You can adjust curves to smooth out motion or add more complex motion.

Create camera animation: Use the Camera Editor to create camera animation. You can record keyframes and adjust the timing and interpolation of your camera movement.

Set up lighting: Use the Lighting Editor to adjust the lighting in your scene. You can add lights and adjust their intensity, color, and position.

Render your animation: Use the Motion Editor to set up your render settings. Choose your resolution, frame rate, and output format. Then, click "Export Movie" to render your animation.

Save your project: Save your project frequently as you work to avoid losing progress.
